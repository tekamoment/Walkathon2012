Tips for the Big Day

Here are some useful tips for the Walkathon at Ragunan Zoo.

  • Preparation
  • What to wear
  • Arrival
  • Location
  • Routes / Laps / Counting
  • Looking after yourself
  • Responsibility

Preparation

Everyone is advised to have a large and healthy breakfast before arriving at the zoo and drink plenty of fluids the night before. The event will start at 7.00am and run for 4 hours, finishing at 11.00am. There are prizes for ‘Early Bird’ participants so try and get to the zoo early.

What to wear

These are the basic things you need to bring and wear on the day. Feel free to bring cameras as well!

  • Your Charity Walkathon T shirt or (if you don’t have one) your House T shirt and shorts or trousers.
  • Comfortable shoes.
  • Hat and sunblock.

Arrival

When you arrive at Ragunan Zoo, you must pay Rp. 10000 to park and Rp. 5000 per person to enter the zoo.

Enter the zoo through Pintu Gerbang Utama.

Right after you go through the gates, turn right. The registration desks will be right ahead.

Students MUST REGISTER before they start walking.

Location

Here is a map of the zoo with the walking route highlighted and marked out.

Routes / Laps / Counting

Students from Pejaten will follow the longer course and Kemang students the shorter loop. Please follow the signs and pay attention to advice from the marshalling team.

If a student from Kemang wants to walk with a Pejaten student or their parent on the longer route they may. They should collect an additional stamp at the half way point so that the recorded distance is accurate.

Looking after yourself

Students may take time out to rest at any point in the four hours and resume walking when they feel motivated.

Free drinks (water and isotonic) will be provided to all participants.

Healthy snacks (1 muesli bar and fruit) will be provided to all participants.

After the event you will need to stretch your muscles. You should continue to re-hydrate and have a long sleep on Sunday morning!

Responsibility

Students are representing AIS in a public place so they need to adhere to usual codes of behaviour and dress. Pejaten students do not need to have a parent accompany them, but of course we would encourage parents to join in.

Kemang students will need to have a parent or responsible adult with them as teachers will be completing duties and are not directly responsible for student welfare.
